Falcon 9

Falcon 9 Flight 20 (Orbcomm-OG2)

#Flight - 20

2015/22/12

Cape Canaveral, SLC-40

Falcon 9 Flight 20 was a historic mission for SpaceX and a turning point in the history of spaceflight. The rocket carried 11 Orbcomm-OG2 satellites into low Earth orbit for Orbcomm Inc., a company that provides global machine-to-machine communication services. While the payload deployment was successful, what truly made this mission remarkable was the successful return and landing of the Falcon 9 first stage back on solid ground at Cape Canaveral Air Force Station. This achievement marked the first time in history that an orbital-class rocket successfully launched a payload to space and then returned to Earth for a controlled vertical landing. It demonstrated SpaceX’s vision of reusability — reducing the cost of access to space by recovering and reusing rockets. The event was celebrated as one of the most significant milestones in commercial space exploration.

Falcon Heavy

Falcon Heavy Test Flight

#Flight - 1

2018/06/02

Kennedy Space Center, LC-39A

The Falcon Heavy Test Flight was one of the most iconic and visually stunning moments in modern space history. This was the maiden launch of the Falcon Heavy rocket — at the time, the most powerful operational rocket in the world. It consisted of three Falcon 9 cores strapped together, giving it 27 Merlin engines and an incredible 5 million pounds of thrust at liftoff. For this demonstration mission, SpaceX chose a unique payload: Elon Musk’s personal cherry-red Tesla Roadster, complete with a mannequin nicknamed “Starman” in the driver’s seat. The car was sent into an elliptical orbit around the Sun, crossing the path of Mars. Two of the rocket’s side boosters performed synchronized landings back at Cape Canaveral, creating a breathtaking moment broadcast worldwide. Although the central core missed its drone ship landing, the mission was a massive success and proved Falcon Heavy’s capability to carry heavy payloads to space, paving the way for future deep-space missions.

Crew Dragon

Crew Dragon Demo-2

#Flight - 85

2020/30/05

Kennedy Space Center, LC-39A

Crew Dragon Demo-2 was a landmark mission for both SpaceX and NASA, marking the first crewed launch from American soil since the Space Shuttle program ended in 2011. The mission carried NASA astronauts Robert Behnken and Douglas Hurley aboard the Crew Dragon spacecraft, powered by a Falcon 9 rocket. The launch represented the culmination of years of collaboration between NASA and SpaceX under the Commercial Crew Program. After liftoff, Crew Dragon successfully docked with the International Space Station (ISS) about 19 hours later, proving that the spacecraft could safely transport astronauts to orbit and back. After spending 64 days aboard the ISS, the astronauts returned to Earth, splashing down in the Gulf of Mexico on August 2, 2020. The success of Demo-2 officially certified SpaceX to conduct operational crewed missions to the ISS, restoring the United States’ capability to send humans to space without relying on Russian Soyuz rockets.

Starship

Starship Flight Test 3

#Flight - 3

2024/14/03

Starbase, Texas

Starship Flight Test 3 was a major step forward in SpaceX’s mission to build a fully reusable, next-generation spacecraft designed for interplanetary travel. The Starship system — composed of the Super Heavy booster and the Starship upper stage — represents SpaceX’s most ambitious engineering project, intended to carry large payloads and humans to the Moon, Mars, and beyond. The third integrated flight test aimed to demonstrate key systems, including stage separation, in-space engine relight, and controlled re-entry. During the flight, Starship successfully reached near-orbital velocity and conducted several critical tests in space, such as opening and closing the payload door and transferring propellant between tanks. Although both stages were lost before recovery, the mission achieved unprecedented progress in flight performance, data collection, and system validation. Each test brings SpaceX closer to achieving its goal of creating a fully reusable space transportation system capable of supporting long-duration missions to other planets.
